# Business Sales Performance Analysis

## Final Insights & Recommendations Report

**Prepared by:** Arya Kansara
**Internship:** Future Interns — Data Science & Analytics
**Task:** Business Sales Performance Analytics
**Period Analyzed:** January 2022 – January 2024

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines **73,100 sales transactions** across **5 stores, 4 regions, and 5 product categories** over a two-year period. The business shows stable performance across all areas, but revenue growth has remained nearly unchanged. The analysis also highlights opportunities to improve profits by optimizing the current promotion and discount strategy.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Overall Business Performance

* Gross Revenue: **₹550.23M**
* Net Revenue: **₹494.97M**
* Total Discounts Given: **₹55.26M**
* Units Sold: **10 Million**

### 2. Revenue Trends

* Revenue remained almost the same in both years:

  * **2022:** ₹276M
  * **2023:** ₹274M
* **March** generated the highest sales.
* **February** recorded the lowest sales.
* Revenue remained stable throughout the year with very little variation.
* **Q3** was the strongest quarter.

### 3. Product Category Performance

* **Furniture** was the best-performing category in revenue and units sold.
* **Electronics** generated the lowest revenue and sales volume.
* Overall, all categories performed similarly with only small differences in revenue.

### 4. Regional Performance

* **East Region** generated the highest revenue.
* **West Region** generated the lowest revenue.
* Revenue differences between regions were relatively small.
* **Store S005** was the top-performing store, mainly due to strong performance in the East region.

### 5. Seasonal Insights

* **Winter** was the highest-performing season.
* Sales were highest during **sunny weather conditions**.
* Promotion and holiday periods did not outperform regular business days.

### 6. Discount Analysis

* **20% discount** was the most frequently used offer.
* All discount levels were used almost equally.
* Higher discounts did not result in significantly higher revenue.

---

## Main Business Insight

### Promotions and Discounts Need Improvement

The company provided over **₹55M in discounts**, but promotional periods generated slightly lower revenue than normal business days.

This suggests that:

* Promotions may not be reaching the right customers.
* Customers may purchase regardless of discount levels.
* Current discounts may be reducing profit without increasing sales significantly.

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Review Discount Policies

Evaluate whether frequent 20% discounts are necessary. Reducing unnecessary discounts can increase profit margins.

### 2. Improve Promotion Strategy

Analyze when promotions are run and which customers are targeted to ensure better results.

### 3. Learn from the East Region

Study the factors driving success in the East region and apply similar strategies in the West region.

### 4. Prepare for Winter Demand

Increase inventory and marketing efforts before winter to maximize seasonal sales opportunities.

### 5. Improve Electronics Performance

Review product pricing, product mix, and customer demand to identify why Electronics is underperforming.

---

## Conclusion

The business is operating consistently across stores, regions, and product categories. However, sales growth has remained flat, and the current promotion and discount strategy is not delivering strong results. By improving promotions and optimizing discounts, the company can increase profitability and support future growth.
